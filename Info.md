# ByteChat Product Brief for Production Web Team

## 1) Product Snapshot

**Product Name:** ByteChat  
**Tagline:** The private social communication layer for campus life.  
**Category:** Campus-only messaging and community interaction platform.  
**Audience Focus:** Students in a closed educational community (currently positioned around IIT Mandi).

ByteChat is built as a student-first communication platform where users can connect in direct chat, join communities, participate in group decisions, and choose how visible their identity is in different contexts.

---

## 2) Core Product Idea

Most student communication today is either:
- too public,
- too noisy,
- too identity-exposing,
- or not built for campus-specific trust and relevance.

ByteChat solves this by combining:
- trusted campus membership,
- real-time chat experience,
- optional anonymity,
- group collaboration spaces,
- and lightweight governance features (polls, moderation, reporting).

The idea is to create a space where students can be social, practical, and expressive in one app:
- chat with known people,
- discuss with communities,
- share freely when identity matters less,
- and organize decisions quickly in groups.

## 4) Target Users

### Primary Users
- Undergraduate and postgraduate students
- Campus club members and organizers
- Peer groups (study circles, hostel circles, event teams)

### Secondary Users
- Student leaders and group admins
- Community moderators
- Active creators and social connectors on campus

### User Motivations
- Stay connected with peers in real time
- Discover and join relevant group conversations
- Speak openly (including anonymously when needed)
- Organize decisions in groups quickly
- Keep communication local to trusted campus users

---

## 5) Key Value Propositions

1. **Campus Relevance First**
Content and conversations are community-specific, not internet-wide noise.

2. **Flexible Identity Layer**
Users can interact with profile identity or anonymous identity depending on context.

3. **Real-Time Social Flow**
Instant messaging, typing indicators, reactions, and active conversation updates keep interactions alive.

4. **Community Coordination Built-In**
Group spaces and polls support practical decisions, not just casual chatting.

5. **Safety and Control**
Blocking, reporting, and role-based community controls provide healthier interactions.

---

## 6) Feature Inventory (Idea + UX Intent)

## A. Account and Onboarding
- Sign up / sign in flow for student users
- Profile creation and profile editing
- Personalized settings

**UX intent:** Fast setup and immediate entry into meaningful conversations.

## B. Discover and Connect
- Student discovery by search and filters
- Start direct chat from user discovery
- Public group discovery and joining

**UX intent:** Reduce friction to start conversations with relevant people.

## C. Direct 1:1 Chat
- Real-time private messaging
- Read status and typing feedback
- Emoji reactions and message interactions
- Media sharing support

**UX intent:** Familiar, modern chat behavior with smooth conversation flow.

## D. Anonymous Interaction
- Anonymous mode in supported chat contexts
- Anonymous identity management
- Identity reveal controls by user choice

**UX intent:** Encourage open expression while preserving user control.

## E. Group Spaces
- Create and manage group communities
- Public/private group dynamics
- Group chat feed
- Member participation and role-led management

**UX intent:** Make clubs, cohorts, and informal circles easy to run inside one platform.

## F. Polls and Community Decisions
- Poll creation in groups
- Live voting updates for active discussions
- Fair participation model (single-vote behavior)
- Poll lifecycle events (active, updated, closed)

**UX intent:** Let groups make decisions quickly without leaving the chat context.

## G. Notifications and Re-engagement
- In-app notification center
- Unread notification count behavior
- Real-time push-style delivery in active sessions
- Action-oriented notification clicks (jump to relevant destination)

**UX intent:** Keep users informed without making them hunt for context.

## H. Presence and Activity Signals
- Online/offline user awareness
- Typing indicators in active conversations
- Lightweight social presence cues

**UX intent:** Make interactions feel alive and immediate.

## I. Safety and Moderation
- User block capability
- Report pathways for problematic behavior
- Group moderation controls (role-sensitive)

**UX intent:** Maintain a healthy social environment with practical safety tools.

## J. Personalization
- Profile image and personal profile fields
- Theme and interface preference options
- Privacy and notification preference controls

**UX intent:** Give users ownership over identity and app experience.

## 7) Example User Journeys (for Website Storytelling)

### Journey 1: New Student Onboarding
1. Student signs up and sets profile.
2. Discovers classmates and joins relevant groups.
3. Starts direct chats and receives first notifications.
4. Returns because conversations stay active and contextual.

### Journey 2: Club Coordination
1. Club admin creates group.
2. Members chat, share updates, and open a poll for decisions.
3. Members vote live; outcomes update in real time.
4. Group takes action quickly with less confusion.

### Journey 3: Safe Anonymous Sharing
1. User enters anonymous interaction context.
2. Shares opinion without profile pressure.
3. Keeps identity private until choosing to reveal.
4. Feels safer participating in sensitive conversations.
